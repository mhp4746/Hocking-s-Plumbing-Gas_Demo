The problem

Why your hot water fails

Faulty pilot lights, leaking gas pipes, sediment build-up, thermostat problems, dodgy controls and worn heating elements — there are a lot of possible faults, and finding the right one can take time if you don't know what to look for.

Even if your water just isn't as hot as it used to be, don't ignore it. Catching the problem early is often the difference between a small, low-cost repair and replacing the entire system.

Service & maintenance

Hot water systems aren't cheap — but the good news is that simply taking care of yours keeps it working at its best for longer.

Cold weather makes your system work harder: the water in the tank cools faster, the incoming water is colder, and we all use more hot water. So it's well worth having yours serviced before and after winter.

Choosing a replacement

With so many options, it's worth getting expert advice. We'll help you choose the right system for your household — here's what we'll weigh up:

  • System type — solar, electric or gas, with a storage tank or instantaneous (continuous) flow.
  • Household size & usage — long hot showers, warm-water washing, and whether you run a dishwasher.
